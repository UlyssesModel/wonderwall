## Intended use

### Primary use case

Cross-asset financial-market regime narration: given a sliding-window
stream of N×N log-return tensors (rendered by Uhura's `cross_section_temporal`
design), produce a brief natural-language narration of the current regime
plus a directional forecast for the next interval.

### Intended users

- Quantitative trading desks running Kavara appliances inside TDX Trust
  Domains
- Compliance / risk teams reviewing regime classifications
- Internal Kavara research validating Pipeline C against Pipeline B and HMM
  baselines

### Out-of-scope use

- **Not a trade-execution signal.** Narrations are descriptive, not prescriptive.
- **Not for non-financial time series** without retraining. The adapter was
  trained on log-return distributions; other modalities (sensor data,
  network telemetry) may produce nonsensical narrations.
- **Not for unsupervised regime discovery.** The four regime labels (calm /
  trending / volatile / crash) are the technical-indicator buckets from
  `scripts/label_regimes.py`. Custom regime taxonomies require relabeling +
  retraining.

- Single-market bias: training data is US equities; non-US markets and
  non-equity asset classes may produce miscalibrated narrations
- Survivorship bias in the historical universe: tickers that delisted
  during the training period are absent from the universe definition

## Limitations and biases

- **Hallucination risk on out-of-distribution inputs.** Narrations may sound
  plausible on input distributions the adapter wasn't trained on (e.g.
  options-chain returns, intraday ETF inflows). Use the HMM baseline (Pipeline
  D) as a sanity check on regime classification when narrations look off.
- **Narration is not factual ground truth.** The teacher LLM that produced
  the training labels can be wrong. Use `regime_correct` (against
  `scripts/label_regimes.py`-generated technical-indicator labels) as the
  factual-grounding check, not ROUGE-L alone.
- **Model assumes square N×N input.** Per Uhura's `cross_section_temporal`
  contract. Hankel embeddings (single-stream case) are research-tier; not
  validated for production narrations.

## Versioning policy

- **Major bump (v1.0 → v2.0):** breaking change to `KirkOutput` contract,
  adapter token order, or LLM hidden dim mismatch
- **Minor bump (v1.x → v1.(x+1)):** training data refresh, adapter capacity
  change, prompt version bump
- **Patch bump (v1.x.y → v1.x.(y+1)):** weight refresh from same training
  config, eval-only changes

Each version's checkpoint records: `prompt_version`, `adapter_config`,
`train_config`, training-data hash. Reproducibility is the contract.
